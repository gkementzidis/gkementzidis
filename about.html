About

I was born in Thessaloniki, Greece. I completed my Bachelor's degree in Mathematics and Physics at Grinnell College, in Iowa. I am grateful for completing my undegraduate studies at a prestigious liberal arts college. The flexible curriculum gave me the opportunity to take courses from various departments like German Literature, Economics, and Film Studies.

My participation in summer research programs in the areas of Mathematical Physics and Fractal Analysis, as well as my experience with Computational Physics helped me develop a passion for Computational Applied Mathematics.

I moved to Stony Brook University to pursue a doctoral degree. My supervisor is Dr. Yuefan Deng. Among other things, I try to model molecules using coarse-graining, reconstruct all-atomic structures from CG, study molecules through all-atomic MD simulations.

Key-words: machine learning (ML), molecular dynamics (MD) simulations, coarse-graining (CG), de-coarsening, multi-scale modeling (MSM), AI-based drug discovery, PINN, CNN, c-GAN, WGAN-GP, AE, VAE, RNN, LSTM, image processing, image segmentation, high-performance computing (HPC), parallel computing, GPU programming, LAMMPS, GROMACS
